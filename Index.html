Pavlodar

Pavlodar is a city located in northeastern Kazakhstan, 450 km northeast of the capital city of Nur-Sultan and 405 km southeast of the Russian city of Omsk on the Irtysh River, the administrative center of the Pavlodar region.

Physically and geographically, Pavlodar is located on the West Siberian Plain.The territory subordinated to the city akimat (administration), in addition to the city of Pavlodar itself, includes the village of Pavlodar (population 4633 in 1999, 5319 in 2009); Kenzhekol rural district: the village of Kenzhekol (population 2848 in 1999, 3978 in 2009), the village of Baidala (population 573 in 1999, 521 in 2009), the village of Dolgoe (population 330 in 1999, 274 in 2009); Leninsky village administration: Leninsky settlement (population 8072 in 1999, 8619 in 2009), Moyylda rural district: Moyyldy village (population 850 in 1999, 810 in 2009) [5]. The total population with Pavlodar is 386,594.The city has oil refineries, chemical, aluminum, electrolysis, metallurgical, cardboard-ruberoid, machine-building plants.

What is Pavlodar mostly known for?

Pavlodar is mostly known for being the hometown of the most succesfull musicians from Kazakhstan Scriptonite and Imanbek.

Adil Oralbekovich Zhalelov (in Kazakh Adil Oralbekuly Zhalelov, born in June 3, 1990, Leninsky, Kazakh SSR), better known by his stage name Scriptonite (Eng. Scriptonite) is a Kazakh performer and music producer, founder of the Musica36 label. He first loudly announced himself in 2013 with a video clip for the song "VBVVCTND", a year and a half later he released his debut album "House with Normal Phenomena", which became one of the most successful Russian-language rap albums of 2015.In 2018, he divided his work into two projects: Scryptonite and Gruppa Skryptonite. As Adil himself explained, this distinction applies not only to recording songs, but also to concerts. So, Gruppa Skryptonite concerts are a musical band of eight people and new arrangements, and Skryptonite concerts are a “rapper + DJ” format and performance with backing tracks.

Imanbek Maratovich Zeikenov (kaz. Imanbek Maratuly Zeikenov; born October 21, 2000, Aksu [2]), better known under the pseudonym Imanbek, is a Kazakh DJ and music producer. In 2019, he became world famous thanks to his remix of Saint Jhn's song "Roses", for which he received the Grammy 2021 Music Award, becoming the first musician from Kazakhstan to receive a Grammy, as well as the first musician from the CIS and the former USSR to receive a Grammy in the category of non-classical music
